Offline

You're offline.

Bharat OS is an on-device-first OS. Most of what makes it useful actually still works without a connection — the parts that need the BE are the ones that read or write live state.

While offline, you can still:

  • Run the on-device SLM (document summary, PII redactor, skill agents) on text you paste in — the model lives in your browser's storage.
  • Browse your previously-loaded shell pages from this device.
  • Read your local personalisation profile (it never leaves the device anyway).

What needs you back online:

  • Publishing a citizen-data offer (signed envelope posts to BE).
  • Submitting an SLM-H skill action verb (calls the BE skill registry).
  • Compute-network dispatch + serve (real-time peer flow).
